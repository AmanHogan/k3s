Business Partner Impact Commitment #1
As a TDP, you will deliver valuable, measurable business impact through your Business Partner assignment.

Goals/Measures (WHAT & HOW):
You will share at least three accomplishments and clearly describe how each one added business value (e.g., improved outcomes, increased efficiency, reduced risk/cost, or enhanced customer/employee experience).

Validation/Completion Criteria
I have identified and recorded at least three distinct accomplishments completed during my Business Partner assignment.
For each accomplishment, I clearly describe what I did, the problem/opportunity addressed, who benefited, why it mattered, the measurable impact, and which value category it supports (improved outcomes, increased efficiency, reduced risk/cost, enhanced customer experience, and/or enhanced employee experience).
💡 TIPS:

Ask your Business Partners: What are the key deliverables you expect to achieve this year? Are there any specific goals or commitments from your organization that you would like me to include as a key result? How can I exceed expectations and make a significant impact (Ex. Time, cost savings, revenue generation, operational benefits) in your organization?
Consider: What are you delivering and how is it adding value to the business? Think about how your work ties back to the 8 ATS transformational program initiatives and 2026 ATS Priorities.
Note: Key results should outline your individual plans for the year and be updated as your deliverables evolve. Track progress monthly during 1x1s with your AD supervisor utilizing the 2026 TDP 1x1 template.
AT&T/TDP Program Impact Commitment #2
As a TDP, you will build your personal brand by actively participating in TDP and AT&T opportunities in addition to your primary Business Partner assignment.

Goals/Measures (WHAT & HOW):
You will provide at least three examples of how you have distinguished yourself and engaged within the TDP program to strengthen your brand.
You will attend and actively participate in TDP experience events throughout the year.
Validation/Completion Criteria
I have documented at least three specific examples of how I distinguished myself and engaged within the TDP program to strengthen my professional brand.
I have attended TDP experience events throughout the year and can list the events with dates and my participation.
💡 TIPS:

Are you serving as a committee lead, or leading a local/national event/initiative? Are you participating in TDP events?
Are you working on any side projects, part time assignments or other AT&T initiatives?
TDP Experience events: For location specific experience events - expectations are to attend in person.
Examples: Participation in TDP Center activities (ex. the Hiring Team, Innovation lab, Finance, AT&T in the News, Beacon, Mentor Pillars program, etc.); Involvement in AT&T Corp activities (EGs, Local Communities); Have you attended any Fireside Chats, Townhalls, or Quarterly Earnings Calls? What did you learn?
TDP Development Commitment #1
As a TDP, you will build track-aligned technical skills, strengthen your AI capabilities, deepen your business and industry knowledge, and enhance your leadership skills - enabling you to deliver greater impact for the business and improve your long-term marketability.

Goals/Measures (WHAT & HOW):
Complete Purpose-Driven Training

Areas of focus:

Technical training aligned to TDP track
AI-focused training to apply AI in work and improve your overall value add and marketability (via Growth Hub)
Leadership/soft skills training to focus on areas such as presentation/speaking skills, emotional intelligence, communicating effectively
Business/industry knowledge training to understand AT&T's goals and direction
Advanced learning (Masters', Nanodegrees, Certifications...)
Complete technical and non-technical training assigned by your TDP AD or Business Partner based on business needs
GrowthHub

Complete all required/assigned GrowthHub training
Make GrowthHub usage a priority; review progress in each 1x1 with your TDP AD
Mandatory Training

Complete all compliance, EH&S, Ask Yourself, and other corporate-required courses
Corporate Success Pathways (CSP): Complete all Drips of CSP curriculum on-time based on your TDP class and assigned timelines
Network Engineers: Complete Network 101 Series in PLE and any additional NE-specific training assigned during the year
Graduation Phase Requirements

Collaborate with your TDP AD to meet all grad-phase milestones (mock interviews, Grad PATH activities etc.) on-time
All TDP participants are expected to graduate on-time
Validation/Completion Criteria
Track and discuss development progress, including GrowthHub, in monthly 1x1s with your TDP AD. Ensure steady progress throughout the year.
Document courses/training completed for inclusion and detailed discussions in 1x1s, mid-year reviews, progression submissions, and year-end reviews etc.
💡 TIPS:

Training Requirement: Mandatory training must be completed 100% by all TDP - failure to complete can impact overall performance ratings.
Skill Enhancements: Reflect on all skills gained from rotations, BP interactions, side projects, mentor sessions, TDP Innovation events and more.
TDP Development (Innovation) Commitment #2
As a TDP, you will complete at least two TDP innovation events or hackathons to demonstrate initiative, collaboration, and the ability to apply new skills to deliver practical, business-relevant solutions.

Goals/Measures (WHAT & HOW):
Innovation participation requirement

Participate in at least two (2) innovation events per year: One (1) between Jan–Jun and One (1) between Jul–Dec

1H 2026

Q2 TDP Hackathon (March-May)
ATS Software Symposium (one of the four) (Mid-May)
Intern Innovation Challenge Coach for an intern team (June - July)
Local Lab Project Team Member + Satisfy requirements (Ongoing)
2H 2026

Bounty Hunters (one of the four) (June)
Q3 TDP Hackathon (Aug - Sept)
Bounty Hunters (one of the four) (October)
Face the Floor (Oct-Nov)
Local Lab Project Team Member + Satisfy requirements (Ongoing)
Validation/Completion Criteria
Hackathons: Completion is validated by end-to-end participation and presenting at the final demos, confirmed by your AD.
Local Lab projects: Project must be approved by the National AD Leads for Innovation. Completion is validated by one or more of the following:
A live national demo with all team members
A 1:1 live demo of your contributions to your AD
A recorded demo of your contributions shared with the National AD Leads
IIC Coach: Participation is validated by successful submission of a patent with your intern team.
⚠️ IMPORTANT NOTES:

Local lab leads: Can use their position to satisfy one of the two innovation events for the year for either 1H or 2H.
July new hires: You need to complete at least one from the 2H26 options.
July grads: You need to complete one from the 1H26 options.
⚠️ Failure to complete at least one Innovation event per 1H26 & 2H26 could impact overall performance ratings.
